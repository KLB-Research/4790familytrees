Family Lifelines

Building your family timeline for PSYC 4790.

This assignment is due on our first day of class. Please bring it with you to the first class as well as to all subsequent classes throughout the semester.

This assignment will help you to build a personal family timeline that will help you to anchor the historical events we discuss in this course with individuals from your own family tree. To complete the assignment, you’ll identify who in your family was alive between the years 1900 and 1950. For most of you, that will probably mean your great-grandparents and great-great-grandparents, though you are welcome to also include your parents and grandparents as well, so that you can see where their lives line up relative to the events in this course. You are also welcome to include other relatives, such as great aunts and uncles, or anyone else that you can identify from your family who was alive between 1900 and 1950.

For each person that you’d like to include, add them using an entry box below. For each person, you’ll be asked to enter their name, relationship to you, year of birth, year of death (if no longer living), birthplace, and where they were during WW2. To find the answers, you may need to talk to your existing family members and do some digging within your own family tree. Websites like Ancestry.ca may also be useful if you are lacking information about the people in your family who were alive in the first half of the 20th century. Do not worry if you are unable to answer some of the questions; simply enter what you do know, and leave the rest blank.

As you enter people, the timeline will build itself on the screen below.

Some students will find that they can fill in many people; others will only be able to fill in a few. Some families have detailed records; many do not. Displacement, migration, adoption, war, and the simple passing of time can ‘break the chain’ of what a family remembers or records. This assignment is just as much about what you CAN enter below as what you cannot enter below.

Privacy: Nothing you enter ever leaves your computer.
