The question this exercise asks is not who were your grandparents, but who in your family was alive between 1900 and 1950. For most of you that probably means great-grandparents and great-great-grandparents — though it could also include great-aunts, great-uncles, or anyone else you can identify from those generations.

Add an entry for each person you can identify. The only thing you really need is a birth year — everything else (where they came from, what they were doing during the war) is what you find out by asking your family or doing some research. Enter what you know; leave the rest blank. As you type, your timeline below builds itself.

You may find you can fill in many people, or only a few, or none at all. Some families have careful records; many do not. Displacement, migration, adoption, war, and the simple passing of time all break the chain of what gets remembered. What you don't know is part of the exercise too — "we don't know" is a real answer.
